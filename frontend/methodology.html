Methodology

What Brain Diff predicts — and what it does not

A comparison tool on TRIBEv2’s population-average cortical encoding. Two texts in → vertex-level predictions out → contrasts, maps, and plain-language notes.

What it predicts

For each draft, the model estimates blood-flow–like cortical activation at 20,484 surface vertices (fsaverage5), then we contrast Version B minus Version A by region and dimension.

Pipeline (short)

  1. Text is aligned to a speech-like pathway (model training constraint).
  2. TRIBEv2 produces vertex-wise responses for A and for B.
  3. Regions use HCP MMP1 labels; seven headline dimensions aggregate subsets of vertices.
  4. Outputs: signed contrasts, 3D maps, radar snapshot, narrative insights — not a medical or individual-brain readout.

Limits

  • Population-average model — not your brain.
  • Not virality, revenue, or diagnostic certainty.
  • Cultural and cohort gaps apply, as with any encoding model.
