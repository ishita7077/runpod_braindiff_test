Why brain data

People are bad at predicting what works on people.

Emily Falk at the University of Michigan scanned 31 smokers watching anti-smoking ads. She asked them which campaign they thought would be most effective. They picked Campaign B. Industry experts agreed. Campaign C was "annoying" and would "obviously fail."

Then she compared brain data to real-world outcomes — actual calls to the quit-smoking hotline after each campaign aired. Campaign C — the one everyone hated — drove the most calls. The brain predicted right. The people predicted wrong.

The signal was in the medial prefrontal cortex — the same region Brain Diff measures as "Personal Resonance." Higher activation there predicted population-level behavior change. Self-report didn't.

Brain Diff doesn't replace focus groups or A/B tests. It shows you a dimension of your content that no amount of asking people "do you like this?" will reveal.

Sources: Falk et al., 2012 (Psych Science) Falk et al., 2016 (SCAN) Scholz et al., 2025 (PNAS Nexus)

Methodology

How it works, in plain English

1

You paste two texts

Any two versions of anything — tweets, headlines, emails, pitches. The tool compares them.

2

Text becomes speech

TRIBEv2 converts your text to speech internally, because the model was trained on how brains respond to audio. The brain regions for listening and reading overlap ~80%.

3

The model predicts brain activation

For each text, TRIBEv2 predicts blood flow at 20,484 points on the cortical surface — the same signal an fMRI scanner would measure. This is the "average brain" of 700+ research participants.

4

We subtract A from B

For each of our mapped brain regions, we compare how strongly Version A and Version B activate it. The difference is the "brain diff." Positive = B is higher. Negative = A is higher.

5

You see the contrast

Dimension scores, a brain heatmap showing where activation shifted, and a plain-English explanation of what changed and why it might matter — with citations.

What this is not

  • Not a prediction of engagement, likes, or virality
  • Not a reading of YOUR brain — it's the population average
  • Not a measure of fear, reward, or specific emotions (those need deep-brain structures we can't see)
  • Not a replacement for A/B testing — it's a pre-test signal
