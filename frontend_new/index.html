The evidence

Thirty brains ranked three ads. The nation ranked them the same way.

Annenberg School Psychological Science · 2012

Emily Falk's lab showed three anti-smoking campaigns to 30 smokers in an fMRI scanner. They watched for activity in one small region — the part of the cortex that fires when a message feels personally relevant.

Weeks later, the team pulled the real-world data: how many people actually called 1-800-QUIT-NOW after each ad aired nationally. The brain ranking and the call-volume ranking matched. Focus groups and self-report had ranked the ads differently.

n = 30 smokers r = 0.87 · brain ↔ national call volume
Falk, Berkman & Lieberman (2012). From neural responses to population behavior: neural focus group predicts population-level media effects. Psychological Science 23(5), 439–445.
