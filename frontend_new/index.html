Method

How a BrainDiff is computed.

Every comparison runs through the same three-stage pipeline. The output is a signed cortical contrast — not a score, not a verdict. A map.

Stage 1
Encode each text with TRIBE v2

Meta FAIR's open brain-response model converts each text into a predicted response pattern across the cortex, trained on fMRI data from 8 subjects viewing hours of naturalistic stimuli.

Tang et al., Meta FAIR · 2025
Stage 2
Project onto fsaverage5 surface

Both predicted responses are mapped onto the standard 20,484-vertex cortical mesh, then contrasted vertex-by-vertex. Positive values = B stronger, negative = A stronger.

HCP MMP1.0 atlas · Glasser et al., 2016
Stage 3
Decompose into seven systems

A Bayesian model average assigns each cortical region to one of seven functional systems mapped from the literature. This is where the seven-dimensional readout comes from.

Yeo et al., 2011 · Power et al., 2011

Not a diagnosis. Not an identity. A lens.

BrainDiff models a population-average response to written content for comparison — it does not read minds, assess individuals, or make clinical claims. It's a tool for people who want to see two versions through something other than their own ear.
